# 09 — Product positioning and copy for Australian teachers

## 1. Recommended product description

> A local-first iPad app that helps Australian teachers review student work, connect evidence to rubrics and achievement standards, and draft feedback for teacher final approval.

## 2. Short App Store style description

> Draft curriculum-aligned feedback from teacher-confirmed student work. Scan or paste a response, review the text, apply your rubric, and receive evidence-linked score suggestions for your final judgement.

## 3. Claims the app can safely make if implemented

- Runs the core grading workflow locally on compatible devices.
- Supports Australian Curriculum Version 9.0 references.
- Helps teachers connect student evidence to rubric criteria.
- Requires teacher review before finalising grades.
- Supports Australian English terminology.
- Keeps AI suggestions separate from teacher-approved final feedback.
- Can be used with content descriptions, achievement standards, and teacher-created rubrics.

## 4. Claims to avoid

- Automatically grades Australian Curriculum work.
- Replaces teacher judgement.
- Certifies achievement against Australian Curriculum standards.
- Works for every jurisdiction reporting requirement.
- Reliably grades handwriting, diagrams, posters, models, or creative products.
- Determines student effort, ability, disability, language background, or intent.
- Uses official ACARA data if MRAC/download integration is not implemented.
- Produces final grades without teacher review.

## 5. UI copy

### Draft grade button

Use:

> Draft feedback suggestion

Avoid:

> Auto-grade

### OCR warning

> Some extracted text needs teacher review before feedback can be drafted.

### Evidence warning

> This criterion has no cited student evidence. Review required.

### Achievement standard warning

> Achievement standards support teacher judgement. Your school, sector, or jurisdiction may have additional reporting requirements.

### Final approval

> Save teacher-final feedback

Avoid:

> Accept AI grade

## 6. Positioning by market segment

| Segment | Strongest value proposition |
|---|---|
| Primary teachers | Faster evidence-linked feedback and local privacy. |
| Secondary English/HASS teachers | Rubric-based written-response review. |
| Science teachers | Explanation/lab reflection feedback, with diagrams deferred. |
| School leaders | Consistent local feedback workflow without cloud upload. |
| Independent/Catholic/Government sectors | Configurable jurisdiction/sector settings. |

## 7. Differentiators

- Local-first privacy posture.
- Teacher-final approval.
- Evidence-linked criterion suggestions.
- Australian Curriculum terminology.
- Built around content descriptions and achievement standards.
- Does not pretend OCR/AI is perfect.
- Handles student diversity through teacher-controlled settings, not inference.
